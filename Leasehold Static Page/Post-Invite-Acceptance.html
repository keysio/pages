Your Lesehold Property Transaction Stages

Before contracts are exchanged and you get your keys, you’ll run through the various stages of a property purchase.

While each property is different, there are a few standard steps to go through. Depending on the structure of your property ownership, some of the leasehold specific steps may differ, but your solicitor will be able to help you with any questions

Hire a solicitor or conveyancer

Once you have agreed an offer on your house, you need to get a legal professional (solicitor or conveyancer) to act on your behalf to handle the legal work to transfer ownership of the property to you. Find and compare conveyancing quotes from regulated and reviewed conveyancing solicitors that cover your area. Solicitors are usually more expensive than conveyancers and are qualified lawyers, so they can offer a full range of legal services. Licenced conveyancers are specialised in property but can't deal with complex legal issues.

AML Checks

Once you have a solicitor on board they will be in touch to check you are who you say you are and that the funds for the property are from legal sources.

Building Survey

There are different types of building surveys available depending on the size/age of your property. These will make sure you don’t have any nasty surprises when you move in like hidden damp, or rotting beams. Unless you are very experienced with property, it is usually worth getting a survey done.

Property and Environmental Searches

These searches are done by the solicitor and will check for things like the land your property is on is not contaminated, or what the likelihood of it is that it might flood. Although some of them are optional, it's advisable to get them all carried out and your mortgage lender may insist on some of them.

Mortgage

Unless you are a cash buyer you’ll need a mortgage to pay for your property and this will involve checks on your salary and an affordability review. You can usually do this directly with a bank of your choice or with a broker who may be able to find you a better deal.

Review the Lease Agreement

You will need to thoroughly examine the lease agreement to understand the terms and conditions, including the length of the lease, ground rent, service charges, and any restrictions or obligations imposed by the lease.

Make sure your solicitor is experienced in leasehold transactions so they can review the lease agreement, leasehold information pack, and any other relevant documentation and advise you on any potential issues or concerns.

Get a Leasehold Information Pack

Your solicitor will usually request a leasehold information pack from the seller or the managing agent. This pack contains essential information about the property, such as ground rent and service charge details, building insurance, maintenance responsibilities, and any ongoing or planned major works.

Arrange an Exchange Date

This is the date that you sign your contracts and agree to the purchase of your property. Pay the deposit to the solicitor - you’ll need to get the money for your deposit to your solicitor ready for the exchange date.
