Hire a solicitor or conveyancer

Once an offer has been agreed on the property, you need to get a solicitor or conveyancer to handle the legal work to transfer ownership of the property. Find and compare conveyancing quotes from regulated and reviewed conveyancing solicitors that cover your area. Solicitors are usually more expensive than conveyancers and are qualified lawyers, so they can offer a full range of legal services. Licenced conveyancers are specialised in property but can't deal with complex legal issues.

AML Checks

Once you have a solicitor on board they will be in touch to check you are who you say you are and, if you are buying a property, that the funds for the property are legal. This will be crucial before the purchase of property can continue.

Contract Information Pack

The seller's solicitor will draft a contract that outlines the terms of the property sale, including any special conditions, the agreed price, and the proposed timeline. This contract should then be sent to the buyer's solicitor for review and approval. This will usually include things like the contract of sale, property information like the fixtures and fittings and the title documents.

Property and Environmental Searches

These searches are done by the buyer's solicitor and will check for things like the land the property is on is not contaminated, or what the likelihood of it is that it might flood. Although some of them are optional, it's advisable to get them all carried out and any mortgage lender may insist on some of them.

This is the date that both the buyer and seller sign your contracts and agree to the purchase of the property. The buyer will pay the deposit to the solicitor - so the buyer will need to get the money for the deposit to their solicitor ready for the exchange date.
