People Involved

There are different people involved in the process of purchasing a property. Here we introduce you to some of the key ones.

At Ferriers we have relationships with a number of parties that are detailed below. While you can choose whoever you want to help with your transaction we always recommend going with someone you know, or you have been recommended where possible so you can be confideent they will be able to do the best job for you.

Solicitor or Conveyancer:

Solicitors or conveyancers handle the legal aspects of the property transaction. They prepare or review contracts, conduct local council searches, liaise with the land registry, handle the transfer of funds, and ensure all legal requirements are met for the sale to be finalised.

Surveyor:

A surveyor assesses the condition and value of the property. They identify any potential structural issues or repairs needed, which can impact the sale price or the decision to proceed with the purchase. There are different types of surveys ranging from basic valuation surveys to full structural surveys.

Mortgage Broker or Advisor

Mortgage brokers or advisors assist buyers in finding the best mortgage deal tailored to their financial situation. They have a comprehensive understanding of the mortgage market and liaise with various lenders to secure the most suitable mortgage product for the buyer.

Mortgage Lender

Mortgage lenders provide the funds for the purchase in the form of a loan to the buyer. They will conduct their own valuation of the property to ensure the loan amount is appropriate and will establish the terms of the mortgage with the buyer.
